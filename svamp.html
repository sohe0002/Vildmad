Chanterelle, common

Risk

Description

The common chanterelle is a beautiful, golden-orange mushroom with a cap ranging from three to ten cm in diameter. The stem is up to a couple of centimeters thick and rarely more than eight cm tall. The cap is firm and often slightly slimy with a wavy edge.

Foraging place

Deciduous forest, Coniferous forest

The common chanterelle grows under trees such as beech, oak, birch, and spruce - and even on heaths, if there are pine trees nearby.

Foraging

Gently twist the chanterelles loose at the base and trim the bottom part.

Season

June, July, August, September, October, November.

If there has been solid rain, chanterelles emerge in large numbers already during the early summer months, but the common chanterelle typically peaks in August.
